Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - born in Maida Vale, London
  • 1922~1926 - educated at Hazelhurst Preparatory School
  • 1931~1934 - studied as an undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge
  • 1936 - published the paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem"
  • 1938~1939 - working part-time with the GC&CS
  • 1946 - was appointed an Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE) by King George VI for his wartime services
  • 1954 - Turing's housekeeper found him dead

I think murder by state agents is unlikely. There is no known evidence pointing to any such act. However, it is a major failing that this possibility has never been considered or investigated."
